Taste the colours

Red

Red foods reminds us of berries and soft fruits, so we antecipate a sweet taste.

Green

Fresh, zingygreen colours are reminiscent of unripe fruit, promising sour or acid flavours.

White

White foods evoke memories of salt and salty flavours, driving the expectations of a salvoury treat.

Taste: A five-sense experience

Our visual senses provide up to 28% of our taste experience. This is a theory Instagram chef Jacques La Merde takes to extreme. Serving up exquisite tasting plates - in fact made from gas station igredientes - to her thousands of followers, she demonstrates how visual techniques affect our perception of food.
